About This Tour

Savannah was founded in 1733 and has witnessed nearly three centuries of tragedy — yellow fever epidemics, fires, war, and countless untimely deaths. The spirits never left.

Your guide will lead you through the historic district's lamplit squares, sharing documented accounts of paranormal activity, tragic histories, and the restless souls who refuse to move on. You'll visit locations featured on ghost hunting shows and hear stories passed down through generations.

The tour covers approximately 1 mile of walking on flat terrain. Comfortable shoes recommended. Tours run rain or shine — spirits don't mind the weather.

Tour Highlights

The Sorrel-Weed House

One of America's most haunted homes. Two tragic deaths, countless apparitions, featured on Ghost Hunters.

Madison Square

Built over a colonial burial ground. Visitors report cold spots and whispered voices in the fog.

432 Abercorn Street

The most photographed haunted house in Savannah. A young girl's spirit still watches from the window.

Colonial Park Cemetery

10,000 souls buried here, many in unmarked graves. Yellow fever victims, dueling deaths, and more.
